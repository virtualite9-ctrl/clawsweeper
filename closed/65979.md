## Summary

Current `main` already normalizes persisted cron job state during load, which blocks the reported startup crash for missing or `null` `state`. The PR's extra direct `printCronList()` guard is not needed for the shipped `cron list` path, which already goes through the normalized service load path.

## Evidence

- **Persisted state normalization helper:** `ensureJobStateObject()` replaces missing or non-object persisted `job.state` with `{}` during cron store load, covering missing and `null` runtime state.
  - file: [src/cron/store.ts:128](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/250acdd2a28c190ed7934e3ba99022f24123d3c9/src/cron/store.ts#L128)

  - sha: [250acdd2a28c](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/commit/250acdd2a28c190ed7934e3ba99022f24123d3c9)
- **Defensive normalization after merge:** When a state sidecar exists, `entry.state ?? {}` is used, and then every loaded job is normalized again defensively before returning the store.
  - file: [src/cron/store.ts:180](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/250acdd2a28c190ed7934e3ba99022f24123d3c9/src/cron/store.ts#L180)
