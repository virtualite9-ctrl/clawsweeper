## Summary

Current `main` already implements restart catch-up for missed recurring cron jobs through a broader startup path, and the open PR is superseded. The shipped implementation also covers the review concern on this PR by avoiding the raw `computeNextRunAtMs(...)` catch-up check that ignored staggered cron offsets.

## Evidence

- **Startup now runs missed-job catch-up on cron service start:** `start()` clears stale running markers, then calls `runMissedJobs(...)` before the normal recompute/armTimer path. That means restart recovery is handled explicitly on startup, not by the narrow `recomputeJobNextRunAtMs` patch proposed in this PR.
