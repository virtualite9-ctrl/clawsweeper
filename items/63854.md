## Summary

Current `main` still hardcodes the WhatsApp watchdog idle timeout to 30 minutes and does not expose `messageTimeoutMs`/`watchdogCheckMs` in the WhatsApp config surface or docs. The issue remains actionable and should stay open.

## Evidence

- **30 minute timeout is still hardcoded on main:** `monitorWebChannel()` still sets `messageTimeoutMs` from `tuning.messageTimeoutMs ?? 30 * 60 * 1000`, with no config fallback.
  - file: [extensions/whatsapp/src/auto-reply/monitor.ts:183](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/5b8bd6371c666a5de43c82305e0315771e8e49d5/extensions/whatsapp/src/auto-reply/monitor.ts#L183)
  - command: `nl -ba extensions/whatsapp/src/auto-reply/monitor.ts | sed -n '170,200p'`
  - sha: [5b8bd6371c66](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/commit/5b8bd6371c666a5de43c82305e0315771e8e49d5)
- **Watchdog still force-closes idle connections with 499:** The connection controller closes the session with `{ status: 499, error: "watchdog-timeout" }` once `staleForMs` exceeds `messageTimeoutMs`, matching the reported cascade mechanism.
  - file: [extensions/whatsapp/src/connection-controller.ts:565](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/5b8bd6371c666a5de43c82305e0315771e8e49d5/extensions/whatsapp/src/connection-controller.ts#L565)
