## Summary

Current main still appears to have the reported false positive. The denyCommands audit builds its known-command vocabulary from the effective allowlist, while the listed dangerous commands are valid policy vocabulary that defaults intentionally exclude. Existing tests cover pattern-like and unknown deny entries, but not the valid-dangerous deny case.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep this issue open and fix the audit vocabulary in src/security/audit-extra.sync.ts. Prefer deriving known denyCommands from a full node-command policy vocabulary; at minimum union DEFAULT_DANGEROUS_NODE_COMMANDS into listKnownNodeCommands. Add a regression test in src/security/audit-node-command-findings.test.ts proving that denying the default dangerous commands does not emit gateway.nodes.deny_commands_ineffective, while typo and pattern-like warnings still fire.
